Margaret Heafield Hamilton

Developed on-board flight software for NASA's Apollo program.

Margaret Hamilton, lead Apollo flight software engineer, in the Apollo Command Module.

Here's a time line of her life:

  • 1936 Margaret Elaine Heafield was born August 17 in Paoli, Indiana,to Kenneth Heafield and Ruth Esther Heafield.
  • 1954 The family later moved to Michigan, where Margaret graduated from Hancock High School
  • 1955 She studied mathematics at the University of Michigan before transferring to Earlham College, where her mother had been a studen
  • 1958 She earned a BA in mathematics with a minor in philosophy
  • 1959 Hamilton began working for Edward Norton Lorenz, in the meteorology department at MIT. She developed software for predicting weather.
  • 1961 Hamilton worked on the Semi-Automatic Ground Environment Project at the MIT Lincoln Lab, where she was one of the programmers who wrote software for the XD-1, used by the U.S. Air Force to search for possibly unfriendly aircraft
  • 1965 Hamilton was the first programmer hired for the Apollo project at MIT and the first female programmer in the project, and later became Director of the Software Engineering Division
  • 1976 Hamilton co-founded with Saydean Zeldin a company called Higher Order Software (HOS) to further develop ideas about error prevention and fault tolerance emerging from their experience at MIT working on the Apollo program
  • 1986 She founded Hamilton Technologies, Inc. in Cambridge, Massachusetts. The company was developed around the Universal Systems Language (USL) and its associated automated environment.
  • 2003 She was given the NASA Exceptional Space Act Award for scientific and technical contributions. The award included $37,200, the largest amount awarded to any individual in NASA's history.
  • 2016 She received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from Barack Obama, the highest civilian honor in the United States
